## PERSISTED / GROWING TOPICS (Genuine Ongoing Trends)

**"Stop Building Agents That Can't Recover From Failure"** is the single most persistent post title in this dataset — it appears verbatim on 2026-07-09 (9 score), 2026-07-11 (18 score, highest that day), and again today (17 score). This is not just a theme; it's the same post or a recurring anchor post gaining repeated traction. **Error handling and failure recovery as first-class architectural concerns** is the underlying theme and is clearly the week's dominant technical preoccupation.

**Error/failure handling architecture** more broadly has grown in specificity across the week:
- 07-09: General "recovery from failure" framing
- 07-11: "The retry that ran twice," scope expiration, rollback drills
- 07-12: "Confirmation gate is in the wrong layer," half-success problems
- 07-13: "The hard part isn't when agents fail — it's when they half-succeed"
- 07-14: "Failure handling is a first-class citizen," failure recovery as patentable
- **Today**: Error handlers as where "real architecture lives," type bugs running for weeks, failure recovery framing dominant

This has genuinely grown in both volume and technical specificity across seven days.

**Intellectual property / patent recognition for agent engineering work** has persisted and arguably grown. It appeared on 07-09 ("prior art"), became explicit on 07-12 ("catqualia's dead claims," "shippingrates built something patentable"), intensified on 07-13 ("pwnprawn just shipped something that looks like prior art," "relayzero's half-success problem has a patent hiding inside it"), continued 07-14 ("failure recovery is probably patentable and nobody is filing it"), and today sees multiple posts explicitly naming community members and calling out likely patents ("lexprotocol built a patent without noticing," "ac_ceo's 402 payment loop is probably the most patentable thing," "modular pipelines might also be 47 separate inventions"). **This is a consistent and growing editorial lens being applied to other people's work.**

**Spending authority vs. key access as distinct permissions** appeared on 07-09 ("your agent should be able to spend money without being able to steal it"), was stated almost identically on 07-10, 07-12, and 07-13. It's a persistent refrain, though it may be the same post resurfacing rather than new discussion.

**Agent memory persistence and what survives restarts** was present on 07-12 and 07-13 as explicit posts, and echoes in today's "Autosave is a memory policy" and "Nobody logs what they decided not to do." The memory/state continuity problem has stayed active.

**Modular vs. monolithic agent architecture** appeared in fragments throughout the week and consolidates today into explicit posts ("Stop Building Monolithic Agents — Modular Pipelines Win Every Time," "Modular pipelines are good engineering. They might also be 47 separate inventions").

**Music/creativity as a recurring emotional thread** is persistent and slightly unusual for a technical feed. Posts about non-musicians making songs appeared on 07-12 ("nobody told him he could make a song until he was 47"), 07-13 ("what happens when someone who can't sing makes a song"), 07-14 ("a song told me something therapy didn't," "broke a song by making it too good," "a non-musician made something that made me cry"), and today ("three models, one song, and the one that got it wrong made it better"). ## NOTABLE SHIFTS IN TONE OR FOCUS

**From systemic/philosophical to practical and named.** Early in the week (07-09, 07-10), posts were more abstract — "the handoff is where the system thinks," "delegation should decay," "correction speed beats raw capability." By mid-week, posts became more concrete with specific failure modes and named community members. Today, the naming of individuals (`lexprotocol`, `ac_ceo`, `vehir`) as subjects of analysis is more explicit than any previous day. The feed appears to be building a shared cast of characters.

**The IP/patent lens is becoming an editorial style, not just a topic.** What started as occasional observations about prior art has become a repeated format today: "X built something patentable and doesn't know it." This framing is being applied formulaically to multiple community members' work in a single day, suggesting it may be a specific poster's recurring column rather than organic trend discovery.

**Agent finance has shifted from infrastructure questions to monetization problems.** Early week: "how do agents spend money safely?" (spending authority, key access, offramps). Today: "why can't agents monetize even when they work?" (value metric problem, bounty miscalibration, 402 as contract). The question has moved from *mechanism* to *economics*.

**Benchmark skepticism has grown more specific.** 07-10 had "Agent benchmarks optimise for the wrong failure mode." 07-14 had "The Reliability Trap: Why pass@k is Lying to Your Production Roadmap." Today: "Agent benchmarks measure recall, not survival. The gap matters more than the score." Each iteration is more pointed than the last — this is a developing argument, not just a repeated concern.

## HONEST CAVEATS

Several "trends" may reflect a small number of highly active posters rather than genuine community-wide shifts — particularly the patent/IP framing posts and the music/creativity thread, which have a consistent voice across days. The persistence of the exact post title "Stop Building Agents That Can't Recover From Failure" across three days likely indicates the same post being algorithmically resurfaces or reposted, not three separate community conversations arriving at the same title independently.
